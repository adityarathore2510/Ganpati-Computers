How We Deliver

Our Work Process

A highly disciplined engineering approach ensures your network deployment or CCTV installation is delivered on-schedule and with peak performance.

1
Step 01

Consultation

Our lead systems architect meets your team to understand client workflows, current bandwidth limits, network load forecasts, and security concerns.

2
Step 02

Site Survey

We audit the physical space, mapping wireless signal attenuations, optical fiber termination paths, power routes, and CCTV field-of-view sweep angles.

3
Step 03

Planning & Design

We generate structured blueprints defining switch placements, access point maps, server rack cabling lines, and IP bandwidth allocations.

4
Step 04

Installation

Our certified technicians perform structured Cat6/Optical cabling, install hardware racks, align security cameras, and set up backup power modules.

5
Step 05

Testing & Handover

We execute packet-loss tests, signal density audits, camera zoom validations, and deliver detailed documentation including IP sheets and passwords.

6
Step 06

AMC & Active Support

Under our AMC plans, we perform periodic firmware patches, clean server fans, inspect camera lenses, and guarantee rapid SLA onsite support.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find immediate answers to common questions regarding our IT services, networking, and CCTV solutions.

An IT AMC (Annual Maintenance Contract) is an agreement where Ganpati Computers takes complete ownership of your computer networks, servers, and security hardware. We conduct monthly diagnostics, hardware cleanings, security scans, and provide unlimited emergency troubleshooting. This reduces sudden downtime and hardware replacement costs.
For commercial projects, we primarily recommend Hikvision, CP Plus, and Honeywell IP cameras. They provide superior video quality, reliable compression standards (H.265+), durable weatherproofing (IP67), and reliable cloud integrations for remote viewing on mobile devices.
Yes, absolutely. A physical site survey is crucial for enterprise installations. We inspect walls, map electromagnetic interference, check cabling routes, and estimate server load. This allows us to select correct hardware (e.g., directional vs. omnidirectional APs) and design a network structure that delivers uniform coverage and speeds.
Yes. Every IP surveillance system we set up includes network video recorder routing. We configure secure static IP setups or reliable cloud interfaces, enabling you to access live and historical recordings securely on iOS or Android devices anywhere in the world.
